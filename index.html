More on Lily
  • Mary Jean ‘Lily’ Tomlin is an American actress, writer, producer, and comedian.
  • Lily Tomlin was born on September 1st, 1939 in Detroit, Michigan to Guy Tomlin, a factory worker, and Lillie Mae Tomlin, a nurse’s aide.
  • After attending Wayne State University and furthering her interests in theater and the performing arts, Lily started doing stand-up comedy in New York City and was eventually able to get her first role in NBC’S Rowan and Martin’s Laugh-In.
  • Laugh-In catapulted her into stardom because of her multiple regular sketch comedy roles.
  • In 1971, Tomlin released her first comedy video called ‘This Is A Recording’ which won a Grammy! It was based on one of her Laugh-In characters, ‘Ernestine’. It became the highest-charting album by a female comedian.
  • In 1972, she released her second comedy album ‘And That’s The Truth’ which was based on the ‘Edith Ann’ character from Laugh-In.
  • Tomlin was the first woman to appear solo in a Broadway show with her premiere of Appearing Nitely at the Biltmore theatre in April 1977. The same month, she made the cover of Time with the headline "America's New Queen of Comedy."
  • In 1985, Tomlin starred in another one-woman Broadway show The Search for Signs of Intelligent Life in the Universe, written by her long-time life partner, writer/producer Jane Wagner. The show won her a Tony Award and was made into a feature film in 1991.
  • Tomlin made her dramatic debut in the film Nashville. She was nominated for an Academy Award for Best Supporting Actress.
  • In 1980, Tomlin co-starred in 9 to 5, playing a secretary named Violet Newstead who joins coworkers Jane Fonda and Dolly Parton in seeking revenge on their boss. The film was one of the year's top-grossing films.
  • Other noteworthy movies that Lily starred in were The Incredible Shrinking Woman, All of Me, Big Business, Short Cuts, Flirting with Disaster, and A Prairie Home Companion.
  • Tomlin voiced Ms. Valerie Frizzle on the animated television series The Magic School Bus from 1994 to 1997.
  • Her television credits include appearances on Murphy Brown, The X-Files, Will & Grace, The West Wing, NCIS, Desperate Housewives, Eastbound and Down, and voicing a character on The Simpsons.
  • In 2015, Tomlin starred in the film Grandma, which earned her a Golden Globe Award nomination.
  • Tomlin currently stars opposite Jane Fonda, Martin Sheen, and Sam Waterston in the Netflix original series Grace and Frankie. Tomlin plays Frankie Bergstein, recently separated from her husband of forty years (Waterston) while Fonda plays Grace Hanson, recently separated from her husband (Sheen). Grace and Frankie become reluctant friends after learning their husbands are leaving them to be with one another. She received her first Emmy nomination in 2015 as a lead actress for the role.
